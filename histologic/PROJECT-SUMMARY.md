# Histologic Universe - Project Summary

## What Has Been Created

This document provides a complete overview of the Histologic science fiction universe project setup completed on November 18, 2025.

## The Universe Concept

**Histologic** is a science fiction universe where history has become an exact science. On a futuristic Earth, all historical facts are stored in massive databases (factorepos), verified for logical consistency, and used as the absolute foundation for truth, law, and governance. Nations are no longer defined by language or ethnicity, but by which version of history they believe. Those who doubt their nation's truth become outlaws, forced to choose between psychological "correction" or exile.

### Core Tension
Perfect order and certainty vs. human freedom and the right to doubt.

## The Dual-Version System

Every major document exists in two versions:

1. **Full Version** (`-full.md`)
   - Complete, detailed information
   - 3,000-5,000 words
   - All nuances and examples
   - For deep work and detailed writing

2. **Concise Version** (`-concise.md`)
   - Key points only
   - Maximum 500 words
   - For context building and quick reference
   - Ensures consistency without overwhelming context

### Why This Matters

When working with AI assistance or managing a large project, you can load all concise versions to understand the entire universe (~2,000 words total), then load specific full versions only for the areas you're actively working on. This prevents context length issues while maintaining consistency.

## Core Concepts Established

### The Factorepo
- Database storing all historical facts
- Every fact contains: time, location, involved parties, event description
- Verified for contradictions against all other facts
- Primary repo (global events) + local repos (personal histories)
- Every citizen's complete life is recorded

### The Judge
- Supercomputer that resolves all disputes
- Accesses factorepo and uses logical deduction
- Decisions are absolute (facts cannot be appealed)
- Each histonation has its own Judge
- International Judges exist but have limited power

### Histonations
- Groups unified by shared belief in specific history
- Occupy territories called histocircles
- Citizens do not question their nation's truth
- Doubt is criminal or mental illness
- Can split (histobranch) or merge (rare)

### Outlaws
- People who question their histonation's truth
- Must choose: corrective facility or exile
- Live in neutral zones or underground
- No legal protections
- Hunted by authorities

### Conflicts
- **Histobranch Wars**: Most common, over disputed facts
- **Resource Wars**: Less common, high casualties
- **Conquest by Division**: Strategic, long-term infiltration

## Project Philosophy

This universe is designed to explore fundamental questions:

- What is the relationship between truth and power?
- Can there be freedom without the freedom to be wrong?
- What do we lose when we eliminate ambiguity?
- Is perfect knowledge worth perfect surveillance?
- Can humans live without reinterpreting their past?
- When does certainty become tyranny?

Every story should engage with these questions in some way, making readers think about the value of doubt, the cost of certainty, and what it means to be human in a world where truth is absolute.

## Success Criteria

This project will be successful when:

1. **Consistency**: All stories fit within established worldbuilding
2. **Depth**: The world feels real, complex, and lived-in
3. **Conflict**: Every element generates interesting dramatic tension
4. **Themes**: Stories explore meaningful philosophical questions
5. **Entertainment**: The stories are compelling and engaging
6. **Uniqueness**: The concept feels fresh and thought-provoking
