- Collateral damage (described well)

**Readers DON'T know**:

- Towers broadcast facts continuously
- Interfaces integrate facts into memory
- Contradictory broadcasts cause the damage

- Why protection methods work

- "They're really bad and hurt people"

**Missing understanding**:
- The complete technical mechanism
- Why it affects humans specifically
- How the system normally works
- Why certain protections work

## Should This Be Added to the Stories?

### Arguments FOR Adding Technical Details

**1. Internal Consistency**
- Provides clear mechanism for all observed effects
- Explains why protection methods work
- Makes weaponization more credible
- Grounds "magic" in technology

**2. Thematic Depth**
- Neural interfaces as control mechanism
- Mandatory implantation as violation
- Can't remove without death = trapped in system
- Technology designed to help becomes weapon

**3. Character Knowledge**
- Scientists (Yuki, Nikolai) would know this
- Resistance members (Elena, Alexei) would understand
- Government officials would know (but lie)
- Gives characters technical vocabulary

**4. Horror Enhancement**
- "Your own brain attacks you" more horrifying with details
- Mandatory implants = no escape
- Always-on receiver = constant vulnerability
- Removal = death = permanent trap

### Arguments AGAINST Adding Technical Details

**1. Pacing**
- Technical exposition slows action
- Readers don't need to know mechanism to feel effects
- "Show don't tell" - effects are shown well

**2. Mystery**
- Some ambiguity is atmospheric
- Not everything needs explanation
- Over-explaining reduces horror

**3. Accessibility**
- Technical details may confuse casual readers
- Neuroscience jargon is intimidating
- Simpler = more universal

**4. Flexibility**
- Vague mechanism allows future changes
- Too specific = locked in
- Room for interpretation

## Recommendation

### Hybrid Approach: Strategic Integration

**Add technical details WHERE IT MAKES SENSE**:

1. **Character-appropriate exposition**:
   - Dr. Yuki Tanaka explaining to someone

2. **Natural moments**:

   - Training scenes (teaching protection)

3. **Gradual revelation**:
   - Early stories: Just effects
   - Middle stories: Hints at mechanism
   - Later stories: Full technical understanding
   - Final story: Complete picture
