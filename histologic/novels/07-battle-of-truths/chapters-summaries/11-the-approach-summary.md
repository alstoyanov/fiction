# Chapter 11: The Approach

**Date**: Early December 2109  
**Location**: Chronos, various secure locations  
**POV**: Elena Volkov  
**Word Count Target**: ~4,000 words

## Summary

Elena's perspective on the same three-week period, showing the calculated operation behind the genuine emotions. This chapter reveals the duality: professional operative executing a mission while unexpectedly developing real feelings.

**The Professional**
Elena maintains regular encrypted contact with Marcus and the team. She reports on Adrian's routines, his access levels, his security protocols. She photographs documents, memorizes facility layouts, identifies vulnerabilities.

She meets with Alexei Volkov (her brother, operating undercover in Chronos) to pass intelligence. He asks: "How's it going?"

"According to plan. He's falling exactly as predicted."

"And you?"

"I'm doing my job."

But Alexei knows her too well. "Elena, be careful. You're spending a lot of time with him. Don't let it become real."

"It won't."

But she's lying, and they both know it.

**The Complication**
As Elena spends more time with Adrian, she discovers he's genuinely good. Not naively good—he understands the world's complexity. But principled good. He questions the fact war because it harms people. He respects Marcus Chen because Marcus chose principles over expediency. He treats Elena (or who he thinks she is) with respect, kindness, intellectual equality.

She finds herself enjoying their conversations not just as performance, but genuinely. His laugh is real. His passion for historical truth is real. His vulnerability when he talks about his late wife is real.

And when he says he's falling in love with her, she feels something she didn't expect: reciprocation. Not complete—she knows this is a mission—but enough to disturb her professional detachment.

**The Calculation**
Elena reports to Marcus: "He's falling. Give me two more weeks, and I'll have everything we need."

Marcus asks: "How are you holding up?"

"I'm fine."

"Elena, if this is too much—"

"It's not. I've done this before."

"Not to someone like him. Not to someone who doesn't deserve it."

Elena is quiet for a moment. "No. Not to someone like him. But that doesn't change the mission, does it?"

"No. It doesn't."

After the call, Elena sits alone in her hotel room. She thinks about Adrian—his trust, his hope, his belief that doing the right thing leads to good outcomes. She thinks about what she's going to do to him.

She pours a drink and toasts to herself: "To necessary evils. And to the people who do them so others don't have to."

But the drink tastes bitter.

**The Access**
Elena begins positioning for the critical phase: obtaining Adrian's access credentials. She mentions her research requires accessing certain historical archives. Adrian offers to help—he has high-level access.

"I shouldn't," he says. "It's against protocol to share credentials."

"I understand. I don't want you to break rules for me."

But he does anyway. Because he trusts her. Because he loves her. Because Marcus Chen taught him that sometimes principles mean trusting good people.

He gives her his credentials "just for the research." She promises to be careful, to only access what she needs, to protect his trust.

She means the promise—and hates that she's going to break it.

**The Guilt**
The chapter ends with Elena lying awake next to Adrian after they've made love. He's sleeping peacefully, trusting, happy. She watches him and thinks about what's coming.

She could stop this. She could warn him, abort the mission, find another way. But that would mean the fact war continues. More civilians suffer. Veridica falls. And someone else would eventually do to Adrian what she's doing—someone who wouldn't feel guilty about it.

So she'll continue. She'll complete the mission. She'll destroy this good man who trusted her, loved her, gave her access because he believed in doing the right thing.

She thinks: *I'm becoming what I fought against. But at least I know it. At least I'm not pretending this is noble.*

She gets up, goes to the bathroom, and looks at herself in the mirror. She barely recognizes the woman looking back.
