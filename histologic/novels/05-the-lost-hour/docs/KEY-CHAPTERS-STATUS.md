- **Philosophy**: Social engineering through certainty manipulation

- **Motivation**: Genuinely believes Histologic protects billions through perfect truth
- **Tragic Flaw**: Thinks protecting the system justifies murder
- **Evolution**: Learns system requiring murder isn't worth protecting
- **Redemption**: Incomplete but genuine—chooses to help build something better
- **Final Position**: "A system that can't tolerate truth isn't worth protecting" (accepts Kozlov was right)

**Wei's Complexity**: He's not a monster—he's a true believer who took his beliefs to monstrous conclusions. His redemption arc shows that even murderers can choose truth over certainty, growth over stasis, purpose over self-preservation.

## Philosophical Themes Established

### 1. **Facts vs. Wisdom**
- Facts are data points; wisdom is knowing what they mean
- Both are necessary; neither is sufficient alone
- Certainty without wisdom is tyranny

### 2. **Certainty vs. Growth**
- Perfect certainty eliminates questioning
- Questioning drives growth and adaptation
- Uncertainty is feature, not bug

### 3. **Trust vs. Control**
- Systems can trust humans or control them
- Control creates fragility; trust creates resilience
- Wei chose control; Mikhail advocates trust

### 4. **Distributed vs. Centralized Truth**
- Centralized: Perfect but vulnerable to manipulation
- Distributed: Imperfect but resilient to erasure
- Human testimony survives digital deletion

### 5. **Redemption vs. Perfection**
- Perfect tyranny vs. imperfect redemption
- Wei's incomplete redemption more valuable than continued perfection
- Systems that can't acknowledge failure can't improve
