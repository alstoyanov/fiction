## Philosophical Themes

### Fully Explored

**1. Memory vs. Record**
- Memory survives when records are deleted
- Distributed testimony more resilient than centralized certainty
- Human witnesses can't all be erased simultaneously
- "History lives in those who remember"

**2. Certainty vs. Truth**
- Certainty can be false (designed authoritarianism)
- Truth can be uncertain (honest testimony)
- Distributed truth stronger than centralized lies
- "Choose honest uncertainty over beautiful lies"

**3. Facts vs. Wisdom**
- Facts are data; wisdom is meaning
- Both necessary, neither sufficient alone
- Machines provide facts, humans provide judgment
- "Truth requires the courage to admit what we don't know"

**4. Individual vs. System**
- Systems should serve humans, not control them
- Good people can implement terrible systems
- Designed authoritarianism vs. corrupted democracy
- "A system requiring murder to maintain isn't worth protecting"

**5. Democracy vs. Authoritarianism**
- Democratic truth-finding messy but resilient
- Authoritarian certainty clean but vulnerable
- Trauma-based solutions become oppressive
- "Opposite of chaos is collaboration, not control"

**6. Growth vs. Stasis**
- Uncertainty enables questioning, questioning enables growth
- Eliminating doubt eliminates human capacity for progress
- Systems must adapt or die
- "Democracy requires eternal vigilance"
